# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to OctoAcme's project management documentation. This folder contains comprehensive guides and checklists to help teams execute projects consistently, communicate effectively, and continuously improve processes.

## Overview of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme operates a structured, customer-centric project management approach organized around five key lifecycle phases: **Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective**.

### Key Characteristics

**Initiation & Alignment**: Each project begins with a lightweight Project One-pager that validates business need, identifies stakeholders, and establishes measurable success metrics. This enables a clear go/no-go decision before committing resources.

**Structured Planning**: Approved initiatives are broken into shippable increments with prioritized backlogs, clear acceptance criteria, estimated scope, and release roadmaps that identify dependencies and risks upfront.

**Iterative Delivery with Quality**: Execution follows a consistent team rhythm (daily standups, weekly syncs, sprint-based work) using project boards and small pull requests (≤400 lines). Quality is embedded throughout with unit tests, integration tests, automated CI checks, security scanning, and manual QA.

**Clear Roles & Ownership**: OctoAcme defines distinct roles—Project Managers coordinate schedules and risks, Product Managers define outcomes and measure success, and Developers implement features and identify technical risks. Weekly PM/PdM syncs and twice-weekly standups ensure rapid alignment.

**Risk Management & Communication**: A risk register tracks identified issues by impact and likelihood; blockers escalate through defined paths (team → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor). Regular stakeholder updates keep all parties informed and enable proactive mitigation.

**Learning & Continuous Improvement**: After each sprint or milestone, retrospectives capture learnings and convert them into action items with clear owners and due dates. Impact is measured and shared to drive incremental process improvements.
